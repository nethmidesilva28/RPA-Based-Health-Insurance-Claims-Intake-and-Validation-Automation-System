# Current State Overview (As-Is Process)

The current health insurance claims intake and validation process is largely manual and involves multiple steps performed by claims officers.

## Step-by-Step Current Process:

1. Claim Submission
   - Policyholder submits claim via email, branch, or online form

2. Claim Receipt
   - Claims intake officer manually retrieves submitted claims

3. Document Review
   - Officer checks attached documents for completeness

4. Policy Verification
   - Policy details are manually checked in internal systems

5. Data Entry
   - Claim information is manually entered into the claims management system

6. Validation
   - Officer verifies eligibility and completeness based on business rules

7. Classification
   - Claims are categorized (complete, incomplete, invalid)

8. Routing
   - Claims are manually forwarded to relevant claims assessors

9. Follow-ups
   - Officers contact claimants for missing or incorrect information

## Key Characteristics:
- Highly manual and repetitive
- Dependent on human accuracy
- Multiple system interactions
- No centralized automation or validation engine
