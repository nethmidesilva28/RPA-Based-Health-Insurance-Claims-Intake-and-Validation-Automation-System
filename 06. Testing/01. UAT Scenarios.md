# User Acceptance Testing (UAT) Scenarios

This document defines the business-focused scenarios used to validate whether the RPA-based Health Insurance Claims Intake and Validation solution meets user and process expectations.

## UAT Objective
The objective of UAT is to verify that the automation solution correctly supports the claims intake, validation, classification, and routing process from a business user perspective.

---

## UAT Scenario 01 – Valid Claim Intake and Routing
**Scenario:** A complete health insurance claim is submitted with all required details and supporting documents.

**Expected Outcome:**
- Claim is captured successfully
- Policy is validated as active
- Required documents are identified as complete
- Claim is classified as **Valid**
- Claim is routed to the **Valid Claims Queue**

---

## UAT Scenario 02 – Missing Mandatory Document
**Scenario:** A claim is submitted without one of the required supporting documents.

**Expected Outcome:**
- Claim is captured successfully
- Policy validation is completed
- Missing document is detected
- Claim is classified as **Incomplete**
- Claim is routed to the **Incomplete Claims Queue**
- Notification is triggered for follow-up

---

## UAT Scenario 03 – Invalid Policy Number
**Scenario:** A claim is submitted with a policy number that does not exist in the policy system.

**Expected Outcome:**
- Claim is captured successfully
- Policy validation fails
- Claim is classified as **Exception**
- Claim is routed to the **Exception Review Queue**
- Error reason is logged

---

## UAT Scenario 04 – Expired Policy
**Scenario:** A claim is submitted under a policy that is no longer active on the incident date.

**Expected Outcome:**
- Policy status is checked
- Policy is identified as inactive/expired
- Claim is classified as **Exception**
- Claim is routed to the **Exception Review Queue**

---

## UAT Scenario 05 – Duplicate Claim Submission
**Scenario:** The same claim is submitted more than once using the same policy number, incident date, and claim type.

**Expected Outcome:**
- Duplicate logic is triggered
- Claim is flagged as a potential duplicate
- Claim is routed to **Exception Review**
- Duplicate reason is logged

---

## UAT Scenario 06 – Unsupported or Corrupted Attachment
**Scenario:** A claim is submitted with an unreadable or unsupported file attachment.

**Expected Outcome:**
- Bot identifies file issue during document handling
- Claim is classified as **Exception**
- Claim is routed for manual review
- Issue is logged with reason

---

## UAT Scenario 07 – Successful Notification for Incomplete Claim
**Scenario:** A claim is marked incomplete due to missing documents.

**Expected Outcome:**
- Claim is routed to the Incomplete Claims Queue
- Notification is generated to request missing documents
- Claim status is logged as pending completion

---

## UAT Scenario 08 – Successful Audit Logging
**Scenario:** A claim is processed by the bot from intake to classification.

**Expected Outcome:**
- All processing steps are logged
- Validation result is recorded
- Queue routing outcome is recorded
- Timestamp and processing status are available for audit purposes
